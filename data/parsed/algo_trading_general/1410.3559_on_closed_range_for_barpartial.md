## 1. Introduction

Extending the Cauchy-Riemann operator, ¬Į ‚ąā , initially defined pointwise, to an unbounded operator on L 2 allows Hilbert space methods to bear on existence and regularity questions connected to the Cauchy-Riemann equations. These methods allow one to deduce powerful results about complex function theory, especially in several complex variables. Results obtained in this manner, after the seminal work of Kohn and H¬® ormander in the early 1960s, are perhaps well-known enough to view extending ¬Į ‚ąā to L 2 as a classical part of complex analysis.

Abasic question, underlying more refined existence and regularity issues, is whether the extended ¬Į ‚ąā operator has closed range in L 2 . In this paper, for ‚Ą¶ ‚äā C n a pseudoconvex domain, we give a general sufficient condition for ¬Į ‚ąā to have closed range in L 2 p,q (‚Ą¶). This condition is not restricted to bounded domains; indeed, this paper primarily grew out of our interest in determining classes of unbounded domains for which ¬Į ‚ąā has closed range. A secondary interest was understanding the closed range property on non-smooth domains. The condition given is sensitive to the bi-degree ( p, q ) where the question of closed range is posed.

We do not consider non-pseudoconvex domains in this paper. For the few results on closed range for ¬Į ‚ąā on (classes of) non-pseudoconvex domains, see [3, 9, 1, 6]. The theory needs more results on general domains, of both positive and negative type.
