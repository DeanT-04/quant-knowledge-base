## Describing the Numerical Range and C -Numerical Range of Matrices via Their Unitary Orbits and the Joukowsky Transform

Ryan O'Loughlin

Department of Mathematics and Statistics, University of Reading Reading RG6 6AX

## Abstract

We generalise the Elliptical Range Theorem to characterise the numerical range of matrices belonging to a subspace of the space of 3 × 3 matrices. Using Specht's Theorem, which characterizes when two matrices are unitarily equivalent, we then provide a novel proof of the Elliptical Range Theorem. Finally, we give an explicit description of the C -numerical range for 2 × 2 matrices and for rank-one matrices.

Keywords: Numerical ranges.

MSC: 15A60.

The Elliptical Range Theorem describes the numerical range of a 2 × 2 matrix as an ellipse. In this article we adapt the typical proof of the Elliptical Range Theorem to incorporate the Joukowsky transform, which consequently generalises to give a new description for the numerical range of a class of 3 × 3 matrices. Then through unitary equivalence conditions (including Specht's Theorem) we give another alternate new proof of the Elliptical Range Theorem and describe the C -numerical range of 2 × 2 matrices and rank 1 matrices.

Although numerical ranges have a long history there is no complete precise mathematical description describing their geometry. The Toeplitz-Hausdorff Theorem shows that numerical ranges are convex and there are now many proofs of the Elliptical Range Theorem, with [14, Thm 1.3.6] being a classical proof and [16,18] being shorter alternatives. Although a modern standard method to analyse the numerical range is based on Kippenhahn's work [15] by computing the Kippenhanh polynomials, despite many years of progress since Kippenhanh's work this is still an involved task, and the polynomials in question often involve thousands of terms. We hope that the alternative method involving Laurent polynomials (Theorem 2.6) presented in this article will contribute to a more practical characterisation of the numerical range, with the recovery of the Elliptical Range Theorem in the 2 × 2 case serving as a promising first step in this direction.

In a similar vein, the C -numerical range of a matrix (defined in Section 4) has no complete geometric description. The C -numerical range of a 2 × 2 matrix was shown to be an ellipse in [22], and a subsequent proof was given in [19]. Our new approach incorporating the Joukowsky transform shortens these proofs and in our proof we are able to explicitly identify the ellipse which is the C -numerical range of a given matrix. The influential 'union of disks' formula was first shown for q -numerical ranges in [25], and has led to substantial progress on study of the q -numerical ranges. With a novel proof, we make a multidimensional generalisation of this formula to rank 1 C -numerical ranges.

One particular reason why one may want to describe the geometry of the numerical range is in order to help solve Crouzeix's Conjecture. This conjectures that for each polynomial p and matrix A , we have ∥ p ( A ) ∥≤ 2 sup z ∈ W ( A ) | p ( z ) | , where ∥ ∥ denotes the operator norm. Crouzeix's Conjecture has gained a lot of traction from the Functional and Complex Analysis communities [2,3,6-9,11,12,20,21,24]. In particular Crouzeix's Conjecture is unresolved for 3 × 3 matrices which have elliptical numerical ranges, which is the focus of Theorem 2.6.

The layout of the paper is as follows. In Section 2.1 we provide a short proof of the Elliptical Range Theorem, and generalise this to 3 × 3 matrices in Section 2.2. In Section 3 we provide a novel proof the Elliptical Range Theorem through the use of Specht's Theorem. Section 4 is focused on the C -numerical range, with Section 4.1 describing the C -numerical range of a 2 × 2 matrix and Section 4.2 describing the C -numerical range of a rank 1 matrix.
