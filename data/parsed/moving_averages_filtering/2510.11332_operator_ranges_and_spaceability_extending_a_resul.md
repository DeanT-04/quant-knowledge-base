## OPERATOR RANGES AND SPACEABILITY: EXTENDING A RESULT OF KITSON AND TIMONEY

GEIVISON RIBEIRO

Abstract. We revisit the results of Kitson and Timoney [J. Math. Anal. Appl. 378 (2011), 680686] on the spaceability of complements of operator ranges, extending one of their main theorems to the general Fr´ echet setting. In particular, we provide an affirmative answer to the question posed in Remark 3.4 of that paper, showing that the conclusion remains valid when the operators act between Fr´ echet spaces. Moreover, we show that the same phenomenon occurs for arbitrary (possibly uncountable) families of operators. The arguments presented here follow the spirit of the original work.

## 1. Introduction

The notions of lineability and spaceability trace back to the pioneering ideas of Gurariy [13] and were later formalized, in their modern form, by Aron, Gurariy and Seoane-Sep´ ulveda in 2005 [4]. Since then, these topics have become an active field of research in Analysis (and particularly in Functional Analysis), with a systematic treatment given in the 2016 monograph by Aron, BernalGonz´ alez, Pellegrino and Seoane-Sep´ ulveda [5]. Over the past decades, several general criteria and refinements have been established; see, among others, Bernal-Gonz´ alez and Ord´ o˜ nez-Cabrera (2014) [6], F´ avaro, Pellegrino and Tomaz (2020) [11], and Calder´ on-Moreno, Gerlach-Mena and Prado-Bassas (2023) [8], as well as more recent contributions in [1, 2, 3, 16, 12, 9, 18, 15].

A conceptual milestone in this theory is due to Drewnowski (1984) [10]: if T : E → F is a continuous linear operator between Fr´ echet spaces with non-closed range, then the complement ( F \ T ( E )) ∪{ 0 } contains a closed infinite-dimensional subspace. This result has become a central tool in the study of large linear structures within nonlinear sets.

Following this perspective, Kitson and Timoney (2011) [14] proved that, for a Fr´ echet space X and a countable family of continuous linear operators ( T n : Z n → X ) n ∈ N (with each Z n being a Banach space), the complement of the linear span of the ranges is spaceable whenever that span is not closed. The authors also asked whether their result extends to the case in which the domain spaces Z n are themselves Fr´ echet (see Remark 3.4 in [14]).

The present paper gives an affirmative answer to this question. We show that the same result remains valid for families of operators T n : Z n → X acting between Fr´ echet spaces, by encoding the family into the range of a single operator and applying geometric arguments in the spirit of [10]. Moreover, we remove the countability assumption and show that the same conclusion holds for arbitrary (possibly uncountable) families of operators.
